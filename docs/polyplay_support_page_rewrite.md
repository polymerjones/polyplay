**PolyPlay Audio Support**

Portable offline experiences for audio, playlists, notes, and reflection.

PolyPlay Audio is a versatile offline space for organizing your own audio into a more intentional experience. Build playlists, compare versions, loop ideas, add artwork, shape the visual atmosphere, and write notes or gratitude entries alongside what you hear — all without Wi-Fi, streaming, or data.

If you need help with importing, artwork, looping, backups, or app behavior, you’re in the right place.

## What PolyPlay helps you do
PolyPlay Audio is designed for people who want their media to feel like an experience, not just a folder of files.

You can use PolyPlay to:
- import your own local audio
- organize tracks into playlists
- loop sections for writing, studying, reviewing, or focused listening
- add custom artwork and visual atmosphere
- compare alternate versions of tracks or mixes
- take notes and keep gratitude reflections alongside your media
- keep your collection portable and available offline

PolyPlay is flexible by design. A meditation teacher can organize guided sessions and reflections. A producer can compare mixes in a playlist. A rapper can present an album with artwork and mood. A comedian can compare different versions of a set. A songwriter can loop ideas and write while listening.

## Core features

### Offline playback
PolyPlay is built around your own local files. Once imported, your library is designed for offline access and playback without relying on streaming services.

### Playlists
Create playlists, move between them, and shape a listening flow that feels intentional instead of random.

### Looping
Use loops for focused listening, writing, reviewing, and repetition. Looping is especially useful when you want to study a section closely, compare ideas, or stay inside a mood for a while.

### Artwork and visual atmosphere
PolyPlay lets you pair your audio with artwork and visual styling so the app feels more immersive and expressive.

### Vibe and Aura
Vibe and Aura settings help shape the feel of the experience visually. They are there to make playback feel more personal, atmospheric, and alive around the media you choose.

### Notes and Gratitude Journal
PolyPlay includes space for notes and reflection, including gratitude journaling, so your listening sessions can hold thoughts, ideas, or personal meaning alongside the audio.

### Backup-friendly ownership
Your media experience is designed to stay portable. Backups matter, especially if your playlists, notes, settings, and local media organization are important to you.

## How to use key features

### Importing audio
Import supported local audio files from your device. If a file contains useful metadata or artwork, PolyPlay may use that information to help populate the experience.

### Looping audio
Set a loop, refine the markers, and use loop playback for close listening, writing, or idea development. Looping is meant to help you stay focused on the exact section you care about.

### Changing the visual feel
Use artwork, Vibe, and Aura settings to change the mood around your media. These tools help turn a plain playback screen into something more intentional and expressive.

### Writing while listening
Use notes or gratitude entries while your media plays so ideas, reflections, and listening can live in the same place.

## Compatibility notes
PolyPlay Audio is built around local-device playback and local storage.

A few behaviors may vary slightly between:
- desktop browsers
- Safari
- the iPhone app wrapper

Import behavior can also vary depending on:
- file type
- embedded artwork/metadata
- codec support
- browser or device storage limits

Video artwork support depends on the device or browser correctly decoding the uploaded file.

## Troubleshooting
If something is not behaving correctly, try the simplest reset first.

### If imported media does not appear correctly
Try importing the file again from the app’s import/settings flow.

### If artwork or playback looks stale
Fully close and reopen the app, then revisit the affected track or playlist.

### If a backup import/export seems incomplete
Make sure the backup fully finished saving before trying to import it again.

### If local media disappears
If the file was removed from the device, or browser/device storage was cleared, PolyPlay may no longer be able to load that content.

---

## Backups and data safety
PolyPlay Audio is designed around local storage. If your playlists, media setup, notes, gratitude entries, or settings matter to you, make backups regularly.

Recommended habits:
- export backups before deleting the app
- export backups before clearing browser/device storage
- store backups somewhere easy to find later, like Files, iCloud Drive, or a desktop folder

Deleting local app data can permanently remove your library unless you saved a backup first.
