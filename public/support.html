PolyPlay Audio Support

PolyPlay Audio is a versatile offline space for organizing your own audio into a more intentional experience. Build playlists, compare versions, loop ideas, add artwork, shape the visual atmosphere, and write notes or gratitude entries alongside what you hear, all without Wi-Fi, streaming, or data.

Need help with importing, artwork, looping, backups, or app behavior?

What PolyPlay Helps You Do

PolyPlay Audio is designed for people who want their media to feel like an experience, not just a folder of files.

  • Import your own local audio and keep it available offline.
  • Organize tracks into playlists that feel intentional and easy to move through.
  • Loop specific moments for writing, studying, reviewing, or focused listening.
  • Add artwork and shape the visual atmosphere around your media.
  • Use Vibe and Aura to make playback feel more alive and personal.
  • Write notes or gratitude reflections alongside what you hear.
  • Keep your collection portable with backups when it matters.

Core Features

Offline playback
PolyPlay is built around your own files. Once imported, your library is meant to stay available for local playback without relying on streaming services.

Playlists
Create playlists, switch between them, and shape a listening flow that feels deliberate instead of random.

Looping
Use loops for close listening, repetition, writing, reviewing, or staying inside a section you want to study.

Artwork
Pair your tracks with artwork or supported visual media so the app feels more immersive and expressive.

Vibe and Aura
Vibe and Aura help shape the visual feel around playback so the experience feels more personal and atmospheric.

Notes and Gratitude Journal
PolyPlay includes space for thoughts, notes, and reflection, including gratitude entries, so listening can live beside ideas and meaning.

Backups
Your playlists, notes, settings, and local media setup are designed to be portable, so regular backups are an important part of using the app safely.

How To Use Key Features

Importing audio
Import supported local audio files from your device. If a file contains useful metadata or artwork, PolyPlay may use it to help populate the track.

Looping audio
Set a loop, refine the markers, and stay focused on the exact section you care about.

Changing the visual feel
Use artwork, Vibe, and Aura to change the mood around your media and make playback feel more intentional.

Writing while listening
Use notes or gratitude entries while audio plays so reflection and playback can live in the same space.

Compatibility Notes

PolyPlay Audio is built around local-device playback and local storage.

  • Some behavior can vary slightly between desktop browsers, Safari, and the iPhone app wrapper.
  • Import behavior can vary depending on file type, embedded metadata, codec support, and local storage limits.
  • Video artwork support depends on the device or browser decoding the uploaded file correctly.

Troubleshooting

  • If imported media does not appear correctly, try importing the file again from the app’s import flow.
  • If artwork or playback looks stale, fully close and reopen the app, then revisit the affected track or playlist.
  • If a backup import or export seems incomplete, make sure the file fully finished saving before trying again.
  • If local media disappears, the file may have been removed from the device, or local browser/app storage may have been cleared.

Backups And Data Safety

PolyPlay Audio is designed around local storage. If your playlists, media setup, notes, gratitude entries, or settings matter to you, make backups regularly.

  • Export backups before deleting the app or clearing browser/device storage.
  • Deleting local app data can permanently remove your library unless you saved a backup first.
  • Store backups somewhere easy to find later, such as Files, iCloud Drive, or a desktop folder.
